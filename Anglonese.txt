# LATIKANA MANIFESTO

An Alternate English using Latikana - Phonetic Edition

## CORE PHILOSOPHY & OBJECTIVE

Latikana is an alternative 1:1 graphic alphabet for the English/Ingliq language that utilizes Japanese Katakana glyphs that completely untethers
Katakana from its traditional Japanese syllabic boundaries.

### The Golden Rule: Zero Epenthetic Vowels

In native Japanese typography, consonants must be bound to a trailing vowel sound. Latikana destroys this constraint
Glyphs represent isolated, raw phonemes. Consonants can be stacked freely (e.g., ST, BR, LNK) without any trailing, dangling,
or orphaned vowel noise unlike many other systems of Japanese symbology for English such as Wasei eigo.

## There is a partner language called Ingliq to Anglonese that exists with equivalents for most if not all Latikana characters,
they are essentially a different "Face" of each other.  Note: long vowels may vary.

## 2. THE LATIKANA ALPHABET - (27 GLYPHS)

### A. Pure Vowels (5/10)

Short A   -> ア (Short 'ah')
Long A    -> アー (Long 'ay')

Short E   -> エ (Short 'eh')
Long E    -> エー (Long 'ee')

Short I    -> イ (Short 'ih' )
Long I     -> イー  (Long 'eye')

Short O    -> オ (Short 'oh') 
Long O    -> オー Long 'ow')

Short U   ->  ウ (Short 'uh' )
Long U    -> ウー (Long 'oo')

## 3. STRUCTURAL GRAMMAR & LIGATURE MODIFIERS

To retain an authentic Japanese typographic aesthetic while keeping English pacing intact, Latikana implements two traditional structural markers:

1.  Vowel Extension: the Line (ー): A horizontal dash that elongates the preceding vowel sound, eliminating the need to write the vowel glyph twice as listed above.  -  Optionally may be placed as a bar (horizontal) across the top or the letter like an accent mark '-' above the character instead - in English this is called a macron accent mark - (Note: in Tategaki form may be placed vertically beside the character)

2.  The Double-Letter Spacer (ッ): Anglonese is mostly phonetic so double letters may not happen, however there is provision for a miniature "Tsu" character to be placed before a consonant to denote a double letter or a sharp consonant punch.

## 4. CONVERSION COMPARISONS & TRANSLATION BLOCKS


### Example A: Advanced Digraph & Stacking Execution

English: Craft

Phonetic Structure: C - R - A - F - T

In Latikana: クラッフト *(Using 'ッ' to punch the transition)*

### Example B: The Modified Sound Cluster

English: Vision

Phonetic Structure: V - I - ZH - O - N

In Latikana: ヴイゾオン

### Example C: Complete Metric Sentence Block

English: "The fast blink"

Phonetic Structure: TH-E / F-A-S-T / B-L-I-N-K

In Latikana: セエ / フアスト / ハルインク

Other characters -
In the related language Ingliq there are optional advanced forms using additional characters for common prefixes and suffixes,
Many may be transferrable to Anglonese however I have not gone there so many other digraphs and shortened extensions are not present.
However, one prefix for un- (possibly also useful for non-, anti-, ir-, etc.) may be represented by a Latikana character: メ

Variants: -

Tategaki - a Vertical form of representing letters (primarily for Anglonese)

Hangulized (Hanglonese) - Block-shaped sets of letters for syllabic representation - note: any otherwise stacked glyphs or characters should be collapsed before applying this design.

Mandala - ring-shaped representation,generally artistic with a large word in the center, and 1 or 2 rings of text surrounding it in a circular shape much like text might look on a coin.

Grammar Variants:
